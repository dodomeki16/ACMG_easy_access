PM3 BP2 cis/trans testing

When two heterozygous variant are identified in a gene for a recessive disorder, if one variant is known to be pathogenic, then determining that the other varinat is in trans can be considered moderate evidance for pathogeniticy (PM3). This evidence could be upgraded to strong if there are multiple observations of the variant in trans with other pathogenic variants.
Finding the second variant in cis would be supporting, though not definitive, evidence for a benign role (BP2).

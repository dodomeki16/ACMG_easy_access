BP7 synonymous variants

There is increasing recognition that splicing defects, beyond disruption of the splice consensus sequence, can be an important mechanism of pathogenicity

Therefore, one should be cautious in assuming that a synonymous nucleotide change will have no effect.

If supported by computational Evidence, one can classify novel synonymous variants as likely benign. However, if comoputational evidence suggests a possible impact on splicing or there is raised suspicion for an impact, then the variant should be classified as uncertain siginficance until a functional evaluation can provide a more definitive assessment of impact or other evidence is provided to rule out a pathogenic role.
